DEBT-TO-INCOME RATIO POLICY

Debt-to-income (DTI) ratio guidelines:
- DTI below 20%: Excellent, strong approval likelihood
- DTI 20-35%: Acceptable, standard approval process
- DTI 35-43%: Elevated risk, additional scrutiny required
- DTI above 43%: High risk, likely denial
- DTI above 50%: Automatic denial in most cases

DTI is calculated as total monthly debt payments divided by gross monthly income.
